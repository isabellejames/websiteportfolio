Course Summary

This course requires students to compose digital media while exploring the rhetorical, poetic, and political implications of multiple writing platforms. Students will learn how to compose a range of critical media objects using web-authorizing languages, text, sound, images, and video in proprietary and open-source software. Classes will focus on theories of writing, composing, design, critique, delivery, and netowrked distributions; critique and analysis of digital media produced by professional and amateur digital media practitioners; and analysis and revisions of digital media composed by the students themselves.

General Education Requirements

  • DSAS Creative Work
  • SCI Expression: Tech/Bus/Res writing
  • SCI Polymathic Contexts: Humanistic
  • Writing Intensive Course (WRIT)

Soundscape Narrative Project

Arrange layers of sound to convey a sense of place and story. The genre of the narrative you convey is open: it could be a documentary, fictional, even science-fictional. The two main goals are to learn how to capture sound and edit it using digital tools and to explore the affordances of sound as a medium, with particular attention to its ability to communicate immersive environment and narrative pacing and change.

Skills Learned

  • Audacity Use
  • Audio Editing
  • Sound Recording
  • Sound Retrieval

Visual Argument Project

Make a rhetorical intervention through the juxtaposition of images and text. It's a collage, but with an argument to make. The two main goals are to learn how to ethically obtain images and edit them using digital tools and to explore the affordances of still images as a medium, and especially their ability to direct attention and help make ideas memorable.

Skills Learned

  • Gimp Photo Editing Software
  • Photo Retreival
  • Text Editing
  • Argumentative Rhetoric

Website Portfolio

Build a multi-page website using basic html and css files, along with any media assets you wish to embed. The two main goals are to learn how to manage a composite project made up of multiple interlinking files and to explore the affordances of the web design stack as a medium and especially its ability to flexibly render content for multiple audiences or reading priorities.

Skills Learned

  • HTML
  • CSS
  • Website Design
